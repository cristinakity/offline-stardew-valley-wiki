Separate Pages for Jelly and Pickles

Why are these the same page? Pickles and Jelly are definitively different items, they have different IDs, generic Pickles and Jelly sold by the travelling cart have different values, they're tailored into different items, they have different gifting tables... they should be separate pages IMO. Sicarious (talk) 10:59, 24 April 2024 (UTC)

I agree - I never quite understood why they were included on the same page. Yes, they're both Preserves Jar products, but Wine and Juice (Keg products) have different pages. I only started playing Stardew a few months ago, so perhaps a combined page made more sense in older versions. Splitting into 2 pages is a big change though, and I think a ton of links to this page will be broken. We'll probably have to wait for a bit until all the 1.6 stuff settles down. Are "basic" Jelly and Pickles edible?

By "basic", I mean the Jelly and Pickles available from the Traveling Cart. In Data\Objects.xnb, both Jelly and Pickles have an edibility of -300 (which means they're inedible). In ObjectDataDefinition.cs, ingredient edibility (or price, if inedible) is used to determine the jelly/pickle edibility. I can't quite figure out what happens if there are no "ingredients", though. This question came up because I've been adding things for the Luau Potluck, and I don't know if basic Jelly or Pickles can even be added. Bluestblur (talk) 22:23, 6 May 2024 (UTC)
