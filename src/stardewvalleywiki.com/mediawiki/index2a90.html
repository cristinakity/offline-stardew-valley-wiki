The Magnet is a type of bait that increases the chance of finding treasure chests by 15% (30% chance base instead of 15% base). It can be crafted or purchased from Willy's Fish Shop for data-sort-value="1000">1,000g after reaching fishing level 9. It can also be purchased from the Casino for data-sort-value="1000">1,000. It can also be found in Golden Fishing Treasure Chests (7% chance).[1]

It is received as one of the prizes for winning the Ice Fishing Contest at the Festival of Ice for the first time, and can also be received as a gift from Willy at the Feast of the Winter Star. It may also randomly appear in Krobus' shop on Wednesdays for data-sort-value="200">200g, or at the Traveling Cart for data-sort-value="15"100–1,000g.

Magnets cannot be used with the Training Rod or the Bamboo Pole. Only the Fiberglass Rod, the Iridium Rod, and the Advanced Iridium Rod can use bait. Magnets can also technically be used in a Crab Pot, but it is no better than standard bait as crab pots never yield any treasure.

Use

Requires:   Fiberglass Rod,   Iridium Rod, or   Advanced Iridium Rod.

Each cast uses one piece of bait or one magnet. When all bait is used up, the game pops up a notification saying "You've used your last piece of bait."

To attach bait to a fishing rod, open the inventory, click on the bait (  left-click or   right-click, depending on how many bait will be used), and then   on the rod. To remove bait,   on the rod.

On an Xbox controller, press   on the bait to select the whole stack (or   to pick up a single one), then   to attach to the rod.

On a PS4 controller, press   on the bait to select the whole stack (or   to pick up a single one), then   to attach to the rod.

On a Nintendo Switch Controller, Press   on the bait to select the whole stack (Or   to pick up a single one), then   to attach to the rod.

Bait can be detached by pressing   on either Controller. (  on Nintendo Switch)

For the mobile version, bait can be added to the fishing rod by opening the inventory, then dragging the bait and dropping it on top of the rod.

Notes

  • Despite the description, the Magnet has the same effect on fish bite rate as standard Bait (decreasing biting time by 50%).
  • While the Magnet can be purchased at the Fish Shop for data-sort-value="1000"> 1,000g, crafting is a cheaper option, even if purchasing the ingredients. In year 1, 5 Iron Ore and 1 Coal cost data-sort-value="900"> 900g at the Blacksmith (data-sort-value="300"> 300g per magnet). In years 2+, 5 Iron Ore and 1 Coal cost data-sort-value="1500"> 1,500g (data-sort-value="500"> 500g per magnet).
  • Success at playing the minigames in the Casino can also yield virtually free Magnets from the Casino shop.
